# Settlement Lifecycle

Settla settles cross-border payments through a multi-step process involving fiat on-ramps, stablecoin rails, and fiat off-ramps.

## End-to-End Flow

1. **Quote** — Settla calculates the FX rate, fees, and selects the optimal route
2. **Treasury Reserve** — Funds are locked atomically in the treasury position
3. **On-Ramp** — Source fiat (e.g. GBP) is converted to stablecoins (e.g. USDT)
4. **Blockchain Transfer** — Stablecoins move on-chain to the destination
5. **Off-Ramp** — Stablecoins are converted to destination fiat (e.g. NGN)
6. **Delivery** — Funds are delivered to the recipient
7. **Net Settlement** — At 00:30 UTC daily, Settla calculates net positions and settles

## Smart Routing

Settla's router scores available routes on four dimensions:

| Factor | Weight | Description |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| Cost | 40% | Total fees (on-ramp + network + off-ramp) |
| Speed | 30% | Estimated settlement time |
| Liquidity | 20% | Available balance on the route |
| Reliability | 10% | Historical success rate |

The highest-scoring route is selected automatically. You can query available routes via `POST /v1/routes`.

## Daily Net Settlement

At 00:30 UTC each day, Settla runs net settlement:
- Groups all completed transfers by currency pair
- Calculates net positions (e.g. if 100 GBP→NGN and 30 NGN→GBP, net is 70 GBP→NGN)
- Executes a single settlement per currency pair
- Reduces on-chain transaction costs significantly
